Dreaming about the United States of America? I offer you the top of best cities for life, work and leisure

This article will be of interest to those planning to live in the United States. Before moving to another country, we think about important things, in which city is the strongest

market, where the standard of living is high and where you will feel comfortable. I want to share the ratings of the best cities in the United States.

10th place. Salt Lake City (7.1 points)

Capital of Utah. One of the most attractive things here is the amazing nature. The city is located along the Wasatch Mountains, where there are a huge number of ski resorts, many parks and a complete sense of comfort. Career opportunities are also attractive. The cost of real estate here is higher than the national average. But living here is cheaper than in other metropolitan areas. Salt Lake City attracts a diverse audience. The center is mainly home to young professionals and graduates of colleges and universities, and in the suburbs, families with children and elderly people are more common.

9th place. Des Moines (7.1 points)

The capital of Iowa is preferred by families with children. Regardless of the technologically advanced life in the city, it is quiet and comfortable in the suburbs. Living in this city is not as expensive as in other American cities. You can rent a house here for as little as $ 800, and buy a house for a little less than $ 170,000. With an average annual salary of $ 47,170, this is more than realistic. And there will be no problems with work at the unemployment rate of 3.5%.

8th place. Boston (7.1 points)

The capital of Massachusetts, with a population of 4.7 million, is diverse. Historic landmarks, tech companies, rich cultural and sports fields. But not everyone will take root here, because this is one of the most expensive cities in the United States. Unemployment is low - only 3.6%, and with an average salary of $ 63,000 per year, you can afford to spend $ 1,230 per month on rent.

7th place. Raleigh and Durham (7.2 points)

Raleigh, Durham and Chapel Hill are renowned for their fields of science and technology. North Carolina's so-called Exploration Triangle lures nearly 80 new residents every day through a constant need for new staff. Also here you can find a large number of parks, as well as music, cinema, museums. Living in these cities is not that expensive. With an average annual salary of $ 51,100, rent will have to pay about $ 910 per month, and to buy your own home you will need about $ 220,120.

6th place. Seattle (7.2 points)

Statistics have shown that it is in Seattle that most Americans want to live. And first of all, thanks to nature and climate. Rains here are noticeably less frequent than in other large metropolitan areas, there are mountains and water around. And the very nature of the city is more calm, not inherent in the 3.5 million metropolis. But if we ignore the natural beauty, life here is one of the most expensive in the United States. The unemployment rate is 4.8%. And if you don't work for one of the top local companies, you will most likely have to relocate to a remote area or suburb.

5th place. Fayetteville (7.3 points)

This city in Arkansas has quickly grown from a small town to a center for higher education, culture, trade and entrepreneurship. Wal-Mart was born here, Tyson Foods and the University of Arkansas are headquartered. Fayetteville residents are welcoming and friendly, and thanks to the multitude of community organizations, you can easily fulfill your social needs. Another undoubted plus is nature. Located in the Ozark Mountains, the city attracts with an abundance of parks, hiking trails, playgrounds and greenery. There are almost 500,000 inhabitants here. Moreover, despite the relative diminutiveness, unemployment is low here (2.9%), with an average salary of $ 43,570 it is quite possible to rent a house for $ 740 a month, and eventually buy a house.

4th place. Washington (7.3 points)

The American capital is clean, there are many parks. Public transport is excellently developed, and thanks to the many restaurants, entertainment centers, museums, sports facilities, attractions and other cultural sites, you will definitely not get bored. But living in Washington is expensive. It is not real estate prices that are creeping up, but the rent is on average $ 400 more than the national average ($ 1,500). And the employment situation is not bad here: the unemployment rate is 3.9%, and the average salary is $ 65,900.

3rd place. San Jose (7.4 points)

The capital of Silicon Valley is a major technology center. The business part of San Jose is built up with modern skyscrapers, the headquarters of Google and Apple are located here. On the other hand, it is an ideal holiday destination. After all, there are a lot of attractions, the ocean and the mountains of Santa Cruz and Diablo nearby, as well as 300 sunny days a year. But keep in mind that the cost of living in San Jose is one of the highest in the United States. Therefore, if you are not an employee of one of the local corporations, you may not be able to afford to live here. Even food and utilities will have to pay more than the national average.

2nd place. Denver (7.5 points)

During the gold rush, Denver was a small mining town. Now this place is a paradise for fans of outdoor recreation and a healthy lifestyle. Cultural life is thriving, with a national beer festival held annually. As for prices, for an average salary of $ 54,450 you can rent a house ($ 1,050 per month).

1 place. Austin (7.8 points)

About 50 people move to the cultural capital of Texas and the world center for live music every day. There is constant musical activity, many creative people, endless festivals and performances. Plenty of parks, great restaurants and sporting events. In addition, unemployment is low here (3.2%), salaries are decent ($ 49,560 per year), rent in relation to income is more than acceptable ($ 1,044 per month). And buying yourself a house in Austin is not such an impossible task at an average cost of $ 265,360.
